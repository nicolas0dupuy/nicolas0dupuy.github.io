About me

Nicolas is a lifelong learner (use the arrows to select your preferred choice). He was born and raised in France in the suburbs of Paris.

He discovered two years after high school graduation that he loves maths and sciences, and got accepted late at Jussieu. He prepared for three years for the admission competition of the Écoles Normales Supérieures, which is impossible to get into unless you prepare in one of the mega top elite Parisian classes préparatoires from where Nobels and Fields Medals come from, and he absolutely failed twice.

He slowed down maths to avoid stopping loving it, and started to learn Russian at the INALCO. Because of that, he needed an extra year for his Maîtrise, but he doesn't regret it (although it doesn't make it easier to explain his background in job interviews...). He then fell in love with language learning. He is now studying Estonian and Swedish more than the others.

He is autistic, you should know. If you meet him and think he is strange, it's good! It means he considers he doesn't have to pretend to be normal around you, and it's a big relief for him because faking normality makes people more comfortable, but it's so much energy that he prefers to spend otherwise.

He doesn't want to be a hero, he wants to be useful, happy, and extremely rich. He has a lot to say, and hopes you will enjoy visiting his mind through this site.

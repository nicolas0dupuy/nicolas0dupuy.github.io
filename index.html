About me

I am Nicolas, I am a lifelong learner (use the arrows to select your preferred choice) born and raised in France near Paris.

I discovered I love maths at 20. I liked them when I was young, but in high-shool I felt like it was no more than more and more formulae falling from the sky. I didn't know before that there are books with demonstrations. I opened an academic math book for the first time because I needed to level up my maths skills to apply to an IT school, but eventualy I filled up my shelves with hundreds of others.
Then I wanted to enter the École Normale Supérieure in Paris, where many Nobel and Fields medalists come from. I was already to old to apply to a classe préparatoire, so I self-trained to compete against top-students from elite Parisian schools to take those multiple 4- to 8-hour exams in maths and physics. I didn't make it (twice). Even now, I didn't move on totally. I frequently improvise some intensive self-trainings as if I was still preparing 20 years later.

I slowed my pace in maths so I wouldn't lose my love for it, and started learning Russian at INALCO. Because of this new language addiction, it took me an extra year to complete my Maîtrise in mathematics, but I have no regrets. Today, I am studying Estonian and Swedish more than the others, but I love exploring many languages, even if I don't reach fluency in all of them.

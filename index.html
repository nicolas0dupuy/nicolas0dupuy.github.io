About me

Nicolas is a lifelong learner (use the arrows to select your preferred designation). He was born and raised in the Parisian suburbs of Île-de-France.

Discovering his passion for mathematics and sciences two years after graduating high school, he gained late admission to Jussieu. He subsequently spent three years preparing for the highly competitive entrance examinations of the Écoles Normales Supérieures—an endeavor notoriously monopolized by the elite Parisian classes préparatoires that traditionally produce Nobel laureates and Fields Medalists. He decisively failed twice.

To preserve his appreciation for the discipline, he decelerated his mathematical studies and began learning Russian at INALCO. This pivot necessitated an additional year to complete his master's degree. He harbors no regrets regarding this detour, despite the structural complexities it adds to professional interviews. This experience catalyzed a deep interest in linguistics; currently, his language acquisition efforts primarily focus on Estonian and Swedish.

You should also know that he is autistic. If you perceive him as atypical upon meeting, consider it a positive sign. It indicates he feels no obligation to mask his neurodivergence in your presence. While performing neurotypicality may comfort others, it demands a cognitive expenditure he prefers to allocate elsewhere.

He harbors no ambition to be a hero; his objectives are to remain useful, achieve happiness, and acquire significant wealth. He has much to share and hopes you will find value in navigating the intellectual space presented within this site.
